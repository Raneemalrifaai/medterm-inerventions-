Three priority intervention locations
Rooseveltlaan / Oktoberplein
District gateway failing WHO Place, Planet and People criteria. Zero tree canopy, no safe crossings, no public space. New Papendorp cycle bridge creates the intervention moment.
Winkelcentrum Nova
Car-dominant service hub with 40+ shops and a health centre but no outdoor public space and zero green within 300m. Active redevelopment process underway since December 2024.
Beneluxlaan
Most critical site. Fails 5 of 6 WHO pillars. Highest noise (60–65 dB), zero tree canopy on a 6-lane spine, no protected cycle lane. March 2025 resurfacing completed without one.
How to use this tool

User manual — works offline in any modern browser, no installation needed

Follow these steps for the clearest understanding of Kanaleneiland's health situation and what can be done about it.

1
Start with the Relationship Bubble Map (01). Fifteen health factors as coloured bubbles — each colour is a WHO 6P pillar. Click any bubble to reveal its CBS score, the gap versus Utrecht, the WHO source, and which other factors it connects to.
2
Open WHO 6P Criteria (02) to see every official WHO Phase VII standard side by side with Kanaleneiland's real measured data. Pillar tabs, status badges, overall 20% pass rate.
3
Open the Cascade Map (03) to trace how each intervention flows through to health outcomes. Line thickness = evidence strength.
4
Open the Intervention Sequence (04) for a step-by-step action plan with concrete actions, cascade effects, and key indicators per step.
5
Open the Full Simulator (05) to test all 20 indicators across all six pillars. Try Urban designers to see only spatially designable indicators, then Highest health impact to see which specific ones — social cohesion, cycle lanes, economic support — move the needle most.
6
Open the Location Simulator (06) to work with specific sites on the real map. Select Rooseveltlaan, Nova or Beneluxlaan → read the specific problems → click through the four intervention slides → adjust each slider and watch the WHO scores respond live.
7
Open Information Sources (07) to look up any specific claim, check the full citation, or understand why a particular causal link was drawn.
