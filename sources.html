Information sources

Every source used in the Kanaleneiland Healthy Cities analysis — organised by WHO 6P pillar. Each source explains what the evidence proved and why it creates a specific connection in the relationship map.

Core framework
WHO European Healthy Cities Network — Phase VII (2019–2024)
The foundational source for the entire map. Phase VII formally defines the Healthy City concept and establishes the 6P model. It added two non-negotiable requirements: intersectoral governance (Health in All Policies) and community participation in decisions affecting health. This is why Governance sits as an enabling condition connecting to every other pillar.
WHO European Healthy Cities Network, Phase VII 2019–2024. who.int/europe/healthy-cities
Prosperity — economic equity
Marmot Review — Fair Society, Healthy Lives (2010)
Introduced the concept of "causes of the causes" — proving that poverty and economic insecurity causally produce poor health through multiple simultaneous pathways. The 15-point socio-economic gap in Kanaleneiland is the upstream driver of the loneliness gap, mental health gap, physical impairment gap, and park access deficit simultaneously.
Marmot M et al. (2010). Fair Society, Healthy Lives: The Marmot Review. UCL Institute of Health Equity.
WHO Commission on Social Determinants of Health (CSDH, 2008)
Established that 80% of health outcomes are determined by social, economic, and environmental factors — not clinical healthcare. The scientific basis for why healthcare access alone cannot compensate for economic inequality, poor housing, or absent green space.
WHO CSDH (2008). Closing the gap in a generation. Final Report. Geneva: WHO.
UNICEF Innocenti — Child Poverty in Rich Countries
Kanaleneiland's child poverty rate of 33.6% is more than double Utrecht's 13.2%. UNICEF Innocenti documents how child poverty constrains nutrition, play, education, and health access simultaneously — creating intergenerational disadvantage.
UNICEF Innocenti Research Centre. Child Poverty in Rich Countries report series. CBS StatLine Netherlands.
People — health & wellbeing
Holt-Lunstad et al. (2015) — PLOS Medicine
Meta-analysis of 148 studies (300,000+ participants) proving that social isolation and loneliness increase mortality risk by 26–32% — equivalent to smoking 15 cigarettes per day. Loneliness ranked above obesity, physical inactivity, and air pollution. With a loneliness index 12 points below Utrecht, Kanaleneiland carries a significant hidden mortality burden.
Holt-Lunstad J et al. (2015). Loneliness and Social Isolation as Risk Factors for Mortality. PLOS Medicine. doi:10.1371/journal.pmed.1001867
BRE/Savills Healthy Cities Index (2023)
Provides the composite scoring framework for the Physical Health Index (59.8 Kanaleneiland vs 73 Utrecht). The 52% physical impairment rate in Kanaleneiland versus 28% in Utrecht — nearly double — is the most striking statistic in the dataset.
BRE/Savills Healthy Cities Index 2023. CBS OpenData — Neighbourhood Health Survey, Table 86270ENG.
Place — housing, transport & built environment
WHO Housing and Health Guidelines (2018)
WHO formally classified cold, damp, overcrowded, and insecure housing as health hazards with direct causal links to respiratory disease, cardiovascular outcomes, childhood development, and mental health. Housing connects bidirectionally in the map: poverty causes poor housing, and poor housing causes disease and stress.
WHO (2018). WHO Housing and Health Guidelines. Geneva: WHO. ISBN 978-92-4-155037-6.
WHO Global Action Plan on Physical Activity (GAPPA, 2018–2030)
Establishes that active travel — walking and cycling — is one of the most cost-effective NCD prevention interventions available. Physical inactivity is the fourth leading global mortality risk factor. The train station at 2.6km from Kanaleneiland directly affects whether residents can incorporate active travel into daily routines.
WHO (2018). Global Action Plan on Physical Activity 2018–2030. Geneva: WHO. ISBN 978-92-4-151418-7.
Planet — green space, biodiversity & climate
PMC Systematic Review — Green Space and Health (2021)
The most important source for the Green Space bubble (27.5 points below Utrecht). Confirmed three distinct simultaneous health pathways: (1) Harm reduction — vegetation filters PM2.5, ozone, and NO2; (2) Mental restoration — reduces cortisol and blood pressure; (3) Capacity building — enables physical activity, social interaction, and community cohesion.
PMC Systematic Review (2021). Urban green space and health. PMID 34470696. International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health.
King's College London — Urban Mind Study (2021)
Used real-time mobile experience sampling to show that mental wellbeing scores are significantly higher in biodiverse green environments. Species richness amplifies the mental restoration effect. Scientific basis for Kanaleneiland's +9.3 biodiversity advantage being identified as an underused health asset.
Urban Mind Study (2021). King's College London Institute of Psychiatry, Psychology and Neuroscience. Proceedings of the Royal Society B.
WHO Global Air Quality Guidelines (2021)
WHO estimates 7 million premature deaths per year from air pollution globally. The 2021 guidelines significantly tightened PM2.5, NO2, and ozone limits. Urban trees and vegetation are the primary nature-based solution for localised air pollution — the scientific basis for the Air Quality bubble depending on Green Space.
WHO (2021). WHO Global Air Quality Guidelines: PM2.5, PM10, Ozone, NO2, SO2 and CO. Geneva: WHO.
IPCC Sixth Assessment Report (AR6) — Urban Chapter
Documents that low-income urban districts experience disproportionate exposure to heat islands, flooding, and extreme weather. Kanaleneiland's position as a low-income district in a flood-prone delta city makes climate resilience an equity issue. Nature-based solutions are the primary adaptation tools.
IPCC (2021). Sixth Assessment Report, Chapter 6: Cities, Settlements and Key Infrastructure. Working Group II.
Participation — social cohesion & resilience
PMC — Social Cohesion and Mental Health (systematic reviews)
Confirmed that social cohesion and mental health are mutually reinforcing — each strengthens the other. Despite all other deficits, Kanaleneiland's social cohesion gap is only 2 points below Utrecht — the smallest gap in the dataset. This community foundation is the asset to build from.
PMC Systematic Reviews on social cohesion and mental health in urban populations. PubMed Central, 2018–2023.
Peace — governance & equity
WHO Health in All Policies (HiAP) Framework (2013)
Requires city governments to systematically assess health implications of decisions across all sectors — housing, transport, economic development, education, and planning. Without HiAP governance, every department works in isolation and investments do not compound. This is the enabling condition for all other pillars.
WHO Regional Office for Europe (2013). Health in All Policies. Helsinki Statement. Eighth Global Conference on Health Promotion.
Empirical data
CBS OpenData Netherlands — Table 86270ENG
All numerical scores in the map originate from Statistics Netherlands' neighbourhood-level health and wellbeing dataset. Published annually for all 2,500+ Dutch neighbourhoods. Includes impairment rates, loneliness scores, perceived health, child poverty, green space indices, and facility distances.
CBS (2023). Kerncijfers wijken en buurten. Table 86270ENG. opendata.cbs.nl/#/CBS/en/dataset/86270ENG
CBS Gezondheidsmonitor Volwassenen en Ouderen (2020/2022)
The primary survey behind the health indicators. Run every four years by all 25 Dutch regional health services (GGDs), RIVM, and CBS. The 2022 edition surveyed 364,557 people. Uses small-area estimation (kleine-domeinschattingen) to produce reliable district-level figures from survey responses.
RIVM/GGD/CBS (2022). Gezondheidsmonitor Volwassenen en Ouderen. rivm.nl/gezondheidsmonitors/volwassenen-en-ouderen

Three leverage points — what all sources agree on

01
Economic equity — the upstream cause. Addressing income inequality and child poverty simultaneously improves physical health, mental health, loneliness, and social resilience. Source: Marmot Review 2010.
02
Green space access — four returns from one investment. Closing the 27.5-point park access deficit delivers air quality, mental restoration, physical activity, and social cohesion simultaneously. Source: PMC 2021.
03
Intersectoral governance — the enabling condition. Without Health in All Policies, the other five pillars cannot connect. Source: WHO HiAP 2013.
